## What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyber attack where criminals send fraudulent emails to steal sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data.

## Red Flags to Watch For

**Suspicious Sender**
- Unknown or suspicious email addresses
- Emails claiming to be from your bank or IT department
- Urgent requests from "executives"

**Suspicious Content**
- Poor grammar and spelling
- Generic greetings ("Dear Customer")
- Urgent threats ("Account will be closed!")
- Requests for sensitive information

**Suspicious Links**
- Hover over links to see the real destination
- Look for misspelled domains (amazom.com instead of amazon.com)
- Shortened URLs (bit.ly, tinyurl) from unknown sources

## What to Do

**If you receive a suspicious email:**
1. **Don't click** any links or attachments
2. **Don't reply** to the email
3. **Forward it** to security@company.com
4. **Delete** the original email

**If you clicked a suspicious link:**
1. **Change your password** immediately
2. **Report the incident** to IT security
3. **Monitor your accounts** for unusual activity

## Reporting

Report all suspicious emails to **security@company.com** or call **ext. 9999** for immediate assistance.
